Health Benefits

Evidence has shown that microgreens are high in antioxidant content, which can help prevent a range of diseases as well as boost mental and physical well-being. Such benefits depend on the microgreens that are consumed. For example, the Brassica, family which includes broccoli, contains vitamin E and a phenolic antioxidant.

In fact, research comparing microgreens to more mature greens, has shown that nutrient levels in microgreens can be up to nine times higher than those found in mature greens. Growing microgreens could be a better way of providing city dwellings with local seasonal vegetables as they are easy to grow at home in a small space.

How To Grow

Make sure to pick high quality seeds and a place with at least 12-16 hours of sunlight.

  • Fill a container with soil, don’t over compact the soil.
  • Water the soil lightly.
  • Sprinkle the seeds of choice evenly on top of the soil.
  • Lightly mist the seeds with water and cover with a piece of cardboard.
  • Check daily and mist seeds with water as needed, to keep the seeds moist.
  • A couple of days after the seeds have germinated, remove cardboard lid.
  • Water once a day while your microgreens grow ensuring enough sun.
  • After 7–10 days, harvest.
